## Software is political

Every login form, every database, every email through a corporate server is a political artifact.

The question isn't *whether* software is political.

The question is **whose politics**.

## What you're actually renting

When you pay Mailchimp every month, you don't own:

- Your subscriber list — you can export it. You'll regret trying.
- Your sending reputation — that's theirs.
- Your members' privacy — that's sold.
- Your templates — when they sunset the editor, you start over.
- Next year's price — they'll tell you in December.

You're a tenant. They're the landlord. The rent goes up.

---

## What is FOSS

# Software the corporation can't take away.

You run it. You study it. You share it. You improve it.

No license expiry. No price hike. No deplatforming.

---

## The four freedoms

0. **Run** the program, for any purpose
1. **Study** how it works, and modify it
2. **Share** copies with your neighbours
3. **Improve** it and share your improvements

— Free Software Foundation, since 1986
